/ 02 — THE STAKES

The story
you don't
see.

Last year, more than 100,000 Americans died from overdose. Behind each number was a son, a father, a friend.

In our county, men who want out can't always find a door. Treatment ends. Family ties are gone. Housing falls through. The cycle wins.

No Half Measures exists because half measures are how good men die.
/ 03 — DON'S STORY

The road
he walked.

Our most important asset. The whole site orbits it.
Don walked out of prison with nothing but a Bible and a debt to pay.

He'd been the addict everyone gave up on. The guy you cross the street to avoid. Then something broke. He went all in.

Years later, he runs the company that pays his bills, leads the church that prays for his city, and now leads the work you're reading about.

No Half Measures isn't a theory. It's the road he walked, built into something other men can walk too.

/ 04 — WHAT ALL IN LOOKS LIKE

One man.
Four stages.
All in.

All in at every one. No conditions. No halfway.
/ 01

In Addiction

We show up. No conditions. We connect men to detox, treatment, and recovery beds — and we stay in the relationship.

/ 02

In Recovery

We build skills. Job training, work ethic, and trades that lead to real income.

/ 03

Transitioning Out

We open doors. A stable place to live during the year a man's life is most fragile.

/ 04

For Life

We stay close. Brotherhood that lasts after the program ends, because relapse waits for isolation.
